# DAT Website — Fast Credibility Audit Through a Faculty Search-Committee Lens

**Prepared for:** Jesse Baxter, ahead of a Tuesday interview for Visiting Assistant Professor / Instructor of Theatre
**Scope:** Fast credibility pass on dramaticadventure.com — not a redesign
**Lens:** A theatre department search-committee member spends ~5 minutes on the site. What impression do they leave with?

---

## The 5-minute verdict

A committee member would conclude that you run a **real, mature, internationally serious theatre company** with a genuine body of original work, named collaborators, a credible board, grants, and an NYT-reviewed production. That foundation is strong and it reads as legitimate.

But the default story the site tells is **"nonprofit founder / artistic director who takes artists abroad,"** not **"theatre educator and practitioner."** The single page that makes the full faculty case — your `/jesse` bio — is excellent but **buried** (not in the top nav, not linked from the homepage). And several pages carry **stale "upcoming" dates and a visible 2021 COVID-hiatus shadow** that make the organization look quieter than it is. A committee skimming quickly could leave thinking "impressive but maybe past its peak," and without ever seeing that you've taught 1,000+ university students.

The fixes below are mostly small and fast. None require a redesign.

---

## 1. First impression (homepage)

**What works:** The tagline *"Craft original theatre, amplify community voices, and explore the world"* is strong, active, and artistic. The mission language (arts education, community service, innovative theatre) is warm and clear.

**What hurts:**

- **A raw screenshot filename is visible on the homepage:** `Screen Shot 2021-05-30 at 3.35.50 PM.png`. This is the single most damaging detail on the site — it reads as unfinished *and* timestamps the page to 2021. A committee member notices this instantly.
- **"TRAVELOGUE is back! Our interactive, travel-storytelling cabaret is now online."** "Is back" and "now online" read as a 2021 pandemic-era announcement (the Clubhouse era). It makes the homepage feel frozen in time.
- **"Over 300 artists"** here vs. **"350 artists"** elsewhere — small but the kind of inconsistency that erodes trust.
- **"DAT is in the house!"** is vague and a little casual for this audience.

**Net:** Mission and energy land; the *currentness* and one unfinished asset undercut it.

## 2. Jesse's professional identity

This is the heart of the matter.

- Across the homepage, Company page, and most of the site, you appear as **"Artistic Director (Co-Founder)"** — i.e., a nonprofit leader.
- The **`/jesse` bio page is genuinely excellent and faculty-aligned.** It explicitly frames you as *theatre-maker, educator, and global arts leader*; names the **MFA in Theatre Arts (Devising New Works), Towson** and **BA in Theatre (Acting & Directing), Messiah**, plus Temple, Gloucestershire, Gaiety, and SITI/Anne Bogart training; and states **"over 1,000 university students"** and **2,500+ young people**, with actor/director/deviser/creative-producer identity, place-based dramaturgy, and ensemble devising. This page alone would satisfy most of a search committee's checklist.
- **The problem: that page is hard to find.** It is not in the top navigation and not linked from the homepage. The only route is About → Company → click your photo. A 5-minute visitor may never reach it, and would leave with only the "founder/AD" framing.

## 3. Teaching & faculty fit

The raw material is strong; it's just unevenly surfaced.

**Supports the candidacy:**
- `/jesse` — covers teaching, directing, acting, devising, applied theatre, theatre for social change, ensemble-building, student-generated work, and global/intercultural performance. Nearly the whole job description.
- `/teaching-artist-residency` — strong applied-theatre and pedagogy language ("lead courses in acting, directing, playwriting," mentorship, underserved communities).
- `/global-play-initiative`, `/past-productions`, `/a-girl` — credible new-play development, directing, and devising track record (NYT review, Jim Henson Foundation, US Embassy grants, Kennedy Center).

**Gaps:**
- The **1,000+ university students** claim is your single best faculty signal, but it lives only on the buried bio and isn't substantiated with named institutions or courses. A committee will want specifics (where, what, when).
- There's no org-level "Education" or "Teaching" framing tied to *you* — the pedagogy story is told as a DAT program, not as Jesse's teaching practice.

## 4. DAT credibility

**Reads as mature and real:**
- 18 seasons since 2006; a real **Board of Directors** with substantive affiliations (MetLife, TIAA, American Indian College Fund, etc.); named company members and international partners; an **NYT-reviewed, Jim Henson Foundation–supported production**; performances at the Kennedy Center, ZIFF, Abingdon, and Divadlo Andreja Bagara.

**Reads as amateurish / unfinished (fix-worthy):**
- The homepage **`Screen Shot 2021-...png`** filename (above).
- **Inconsistent stats across pages:** artists 300 vs. 350; plays "30+" vs. "32"; students "2,000" vs. "2,500"; "27 showcases" vs. "75+ mingas & showcases."
- **"Folder: Programs" points to `/new-folder`** — an unnamed default Squarespace slug in the navigation structure.
- On **Who We Are**, several links (the plays, *A Girl without Wings*, *The Rebel Bird*) appear to point to broken `applewebdata://` addresses rather than real pages — worth verifying and fixing.
- **Program-name inconsistency:** the Travel Abroad page describes a program called **"SITE-LINES,"** while the nav lists **"PASSAGE Projects."** Pick one name.

## 5. Currentness (the biggest risk cluster)

Several pages present **past dates as "upcoming"** and surface a long COVID gap:

- **`/action`** headlines *"Upcoming Expeditions: July 10 – August 20, 2024"* — two years past — even though the list below it shows 2025–2028 trips. The prominent date is stale.
- **`/teaching-artist-residency`** headlines *"Upcoming Residency: The Galápagos Residency, March 22 – April 2, 2026"* — already past — alongside a live *"We're currently seeking Teaching Artists"* call and a booking link. This looks abandoned.
- **`/past-productions`** lists Season 18 (2023/24) as the most recent, with Seasons 14–17 marked **"COVID-19 Hiatus."** There is **no 2024/25 or 2025/26 season** shown. To a quick reader, the company looks dormant since ~2023.
- **`/global-play-initiative`** ends at **"2020–2023 COVID Hiatus,"** with nothing newer.
- Homepage **Travelogue "is back / now online"** + Clubhouse-era framing.

The truth (per `/action`) is that you have trips running *through 2028* and a Tanzania expedition this very month — but the prominent, dated copy hides that. **This is the gap most likely to create a false "past its peak" impression.**

## 6. Navigation risk — likely committee clicks

A committee member will most likely click: **About → Who We Are**, **Company**, one or two **Programs**, **Past Productions**, and maybe **Contact**.

- **Strengthens candidacy:** `/company` (real team + board), `/past-productions` (breadth of original work), `/a-girl` (NYT-reviewed, full credits), `/global-play-initiative` (devising/new-play depth) — and `/jesse` *if they find it*.
- **Weakens / confuses:** `/action` and `/teaching-artist-residency` (stale "upcoming" dates), the homepage screenshot, `/alumni` (a long, out-of-order, season-jumbled list that reads as disorganized), and the **`stories.dramaticadventure.com` profile pages** — your "View Profile" link and the board members' bio links all render to a **"Loading…" spinner / blank page** when fetched, suggesting they may not load reliably for a visitor. Worth testing live.
- **Structural risk:** because `/jesse` isn't in the nav, the page that wins the interview is the one a committee is least likely to see.

## 7. Suggested language fixes (small, targeted — no rewrite)

- **Homepage hero/Travelogue block:** replace *"TRAVELOGUE is back! …now online"* with a current line, e.g. *"Travelogue — our live travel-storytelling cabaret. Season 8 returns to NYC [date]."* Remove the Clubhouse reference.
- **Homepage stat:** change "Over 300 artists" to match the **350** used elsewhere (and use one number sitewide).
- **`/action`:** delete or relabel the *"July 10 – August 20, 2024"* headline; lead with the next real expedition (Tanzania, July 2026).
- **`/teaching-artist-residency`:** mark the March 2026 Galápagos residency as **past/completed**, or replace with the next residency; pause the "currently seeking" call if it's closed.
- **`/past-productions`:** add a brief current line (e.g., a 2025/26 season note or "2026 expeditions in progress") so the page doesn't end on COVID.
- **Add an educator line to the homepage or About,** e.g. *"Founded and led by MFA-trained theatre-maker and educator Jesse Baxter,"* linking to `/jesse`.
