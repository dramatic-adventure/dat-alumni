Mockup v9 · Three stations

Zurich, London, Utrecht

Three different boards from three different stations — the Swiss Solari with white flaps and platform chips, the UK amber dot-matrix screen, and the Dutch yellow enamel plate with one giant flapping word.

Option 1 · Zürich HB

The Zurich

Classic Solari grammar: white flap cells on charcoal, a thin red rule under the header, and yellow platform chips as the buttons — each chip opens that journey, the newest chip runs pink with a pulse. Column heads like a real departure hall.

Option 2 · King's Cross

The London

The National Rail dot-matrix: amber LED lines on a black screen in a plastic bezel, scanlines and glow, platform reading in green, and the whole record scrolling past on the calling-at line. The blinking cursor keeps it alive even with one journey.

Option 3 · the yellow enamel plate

The Enamel

Dutch-station yellow — the brand's own yellow — as an enamel plate with corner screws. One giant Anton destination flaps through the record; the journeys ride below as ink tab buttons, the current one pink. Loudest and most graphic of the three.
